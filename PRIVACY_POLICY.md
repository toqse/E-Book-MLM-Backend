## 1. Introduction

By creating an account, completing KYC, purchasing an e-book, or otherwise using the Platform, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agreed to the practices described in this Policy.

## 2. Definitions

- **"Personal Data"** means any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data, as defined under the DPDP Act, 2023.
- **"Sensitive Personal Data or Information" ("SPDI")** has the meaning ascribed under the SPDI Rules, 2011, and includes financial information, biometric data, and identification documents.
- **"Data Principal"** means the individual to whom the Personal Data relates (i.e., you).
- **"Data Fiduciary"** means the entity that determines the purpose and means of processing Personal Data (i.e., Just 200).
- **"KYC"** means Know Your Customer verification, including identity, address, and bank verification.
- **"MLM"** means the multi-level / direct-selling commission structure operated by the Platform.

## 8. Data Security

We implement **reasonable security practices and procedures** commensurate with the sensitivity of the data processed, in line with the SPDI Rules and ISO/IEC 27001 principles, including:

- TLS / HTTPS encryption for all data in transit
- Encryption at rest for KYC documents, bank details, and e-book content stored on Azure
- Hashed and salted password storage (Django PBKDF2/Argon2)
- Time-bound JWT access tokens with strict expiry
- OTP-based two-factor verification for sensitive actions (login, agreement acceptance)
- Role-based access controls (SUPER_ADMIN, FINANCE, SUPPORT) and the principle of least privilege
- Application-level throttling and rate-limiting to mitigate brute-force attacks
- Audit logging of all administrative actions and KYC reviews
- Regular vulnerability assessment, penetration testing, and security patching
- Background checks and confidentiality agreements for personnel with data access

Despite these safeguards, no internet-based system can be guaranteed to be 100% secure. You are responsible for keeping your login credentials, OTPs, and device confidential. **Notify us immediately at the contact address in Section 15 if you suspect unauthorised access to your account.**

In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the **Data Protection Board of India** and affected Data Principals without undue delay, in accordance with Section 8(6) of the DPDP Act, 2023.

## 9. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDP Act, 2023 you have the following rights, subject to verification of identity:

1. **Right to access** — obtain a summary of the Personal Data we hold about you and the processing activities.
2. **Right to correction and erasure** — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data, and erasure where the data is no longer necessary.
3. **Right to grievance redressal** — raise complaints with our Grievance Officer (Section 15).
4. **Right to nominate** — nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity.
5. **Right to withdraw consent** — withdraw consent at any time, where processing is based solely on consent. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
6. **Right to data portability** (where technically feasible) — receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.

To exercise any of these rights, write to our Grievance Officer at the address provided in Section 15. We will respond within **30 days** of receipt of a valid request.

> **Note:** Certain data (KYC records, tax invoices, AML/CFT records) cannot be erased prior to the expiry of statutory retention periods even upon request.
